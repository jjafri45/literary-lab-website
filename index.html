Author FAQ

Questions Indie Authors Ask
Before They Inquire

These FAQs are written around self-publishing search intent: formatting, KDP readiness, cover design, revisions, and what first-time authors need to prepare.

What does a book formatter do for self-publishing authors?
A professional formatter turns a manuscript into a readable, print-ready interior. That includes margins, headers, page numbers, chapter structure, trim-size setup, and the PDF or eBook files needed for platforms like Amazon KDP and IngramSpark.
Do I need a professional book cover for Amazon KDP?
Yes, especially if you want cold traffic to take the book seriously. On Amazon, your cover has to work at thumbnail size first. Strong genre fit, readable typography, and compliant print specs all affect whether a browser becomes a buyer.
Can you format my manuscript for both KDP and IngramSpark?
Yes. We prepare interiors and covers for Amazon KDP, IngramSpark, and related print-on-demand workflows. That includes trim-size planning, bleed, spine setup, and export settings that match the platform you are publishing to.
What files do I need before I publish my book on Amazon?
Most authors need a print-ready interior PDF, a compliant cover file, and in many cases a separate Kindle or EPUB file. You also need finalized metadata such as title, subtitle, author name, trim size, and description.
How much does book formatting and cover design cost?
It depends on scope, but authors usually want a simple answer: what is included, how many formats are covered, and whether revisions are limited. That is why we use flat project pricing and clarify deliverables before work starts.
Can you help if my manuscript is still rough?
Yes. Some clients come in with final text, but many arrive with a rough manuscript. We can support proofreading, light editing, design, formatting, and publishing prep so the project moves from draft to platform-prepared files in one workflow.
